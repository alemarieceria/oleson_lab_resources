How Does the Oleson Lab Work with Data?

Published

December 23, 2023

Modified

December 23, 2023

Overview of Projects and Data Types

  1. What are our previous, current, and future projects?
  2. What types of data have we collected, and what do we plan to collect?
  3. Are there new types of data or data sources we are considering exploring?

Data Storage, Organization, and Tracking

  1. How and where is our data currently stored (both physically and digitally)?
  2. What are our protocols for naming files and organizing directories?
  3. Do we have a centralized system or database for tracking our data?
  4. How do we ensure the security and confidentiality of our data?
  5. What are our backup and disaster recovery plans for data?

Documentation, Metadata, and Data Quality

  1. How do we document our datasets, and where are these documents stored?
  2. What standards do we follow for creating and maintaining data dictionaries and metadata?
  3. How do we ensure the quality and integrity of our data?
  4. Are there processes in place for regular data cleaning and validation?
  5. How do we currently document our data analysis workflows? Are these documents easily accessible and understandable to all team members, including new ones?

Tools, Software, and Formats

  1. What software and tools are currently used for data exploration and analysis?
  2. Are team members proficient in these tools, or is training required?
  3. What common data formats do we use for ease of sharing and collaboration?
  4. How do we ensure software and tool compatibility across different team members?

Data Ownership, Access, and Sharing

  1. Who are the primary data owners and maintainers for each project?
  2. How is data access controlled within the lab?
  3. What are our protocols for sharing data with external collaborators?
  4. Are we complying with all relevant data sharing policies and guidelines?

Version Control, Analysis Documentation, and Training

  1. How do we document our analysis methods and software settings?
  2. Is there a need for a workshop or training session on tools like Git, GitHub, renv R package, Conda, or Mamba for version control?
  3. What version control systems are we currently using?

Compliance, Ethics, and Interdisciplinary Collaboration

  1. Are we adhering to ethical guidelines and legal requirements related to data (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA)?
  2. How do we handle sensitive or confidential data?
  3. Are our data management practices adaptable for interdisciplinary collaborations?

Long-term Management, Feedback, and Resource Allocation

  1. How do we plan for the long-term storage, preservation, and archiving of our data?
  2. What challenges have we encountered in data management, and how can we improve?
  3. Do we have adequate resources (hardware, software, personnel) for effective data management?

Miscellaneous and Continuous Improvement

  1. Are there any specific data management tools or practices we should consider adopting?
    • DMPTool
  2. How frequently should we review and update our data management policies?
  3. What training or resources would help improve our data management practices?
